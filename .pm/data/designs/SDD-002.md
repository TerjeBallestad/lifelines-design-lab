# Desk-to-apartment intake loop: Grete as infrastructure

## Question

Can the React design lab prototype the first playable Lifelines arc as a linked casework loop — **Desk → Frank action → obscured human contact → document/report → new action/time choice → apartment evidence → desk interpretation** — before Grete dies and the same systems become unreliable?

This SDD replaces the current “phone practice lab as the whole slice” framing with a broader intake loop. Phone practice remains important later, but the next iteration should first prove that the player understands the case through documents, Frank, time, and apartment evidence.

## Core hypothesis

The pre-Grete-death sequence works if the player gradually discovers that Grete is the hidden infrastructure of the apartment.

Before Grete dies:

- Grete answers the phone.
- Grete mediates Elling.
- Grete keeps the apartment socially and practically legible.
- The desk can still ask questions through her.
- The player sees doubt, not yet catastrophe.

After Grete dies:

- The same actions still exist, but their reliability breaks.
- Ringing Grete no longer works.
- Frank must reach Elling directly.
- Unopened mail becomes threat rather than texture.
- Financial documents stop being background paperwork and become a reason the apartment may be lost.
- The player must either support/build Elling toward workable independence or decide relocation is necessary.

The loop should not tell the player “Elling cannot take care of himself” too early. It should show contradictions:

- Elling says he is fine; the fridge disagrees.
- Grete says he is a smart boy; the financial statement shows he may not be able to hold the apartment alone.
- The player requests documents; later those same documents sit unopened on the kitchen counter.
- Frank reports polite contact; the apartment shows who did not participate.

## Design law

> Help is not a buff. Help changes the ecology.

The first arc should teach this through casework, not a tutorial popup. Every action changes what can be seen, what is asked of Frank, and what pressure the apartment is under.

## Player-facing spine

```text
Bekymringsmelding
→ Desk with one document and Frank figurine
→ Click Frank
→ Ring Grete
→ Obscured phone scene
→ Frank report + recommendations
→ Spend action dice/time on casework actions
→ Request financial statements and/or plan social visit
→ Advance day when resources are spent or by choice
→ New day: fresh action dice/time
→ Frank social visit opens apartment view
→ Player sees coffee visit, obscured conversation, Elling at distance, mail/documents on counter
→ Frank returns to desk with report
→ Frank chat unlocks for questions/recommendations
→ Observe mechanic unlocks: spend tokens to observe apartment in god-view
→ Grete dies / becomes unreachable
→ Same loop tightens: support/build up or relocate
```

## Target player feeling

The intended feeling is not mastery. It is cautious casework.

The player should feel:

1. **Initial clarity:** “I have one document and one obvious action.”
2. **Human uncertainty:** “Grete sounds reachable, but Elling remains blurry.”
3. **Bureaucratic pressure:** “Money, lease, mail, and deadlines matter.”
4. **Connected consequence:** “The documents I requested now exist in the apartment as objects.”
5. **Professional doubt:** “Frank saw more, but still cannot conclude everything.”
6. **State gaze discomfort:** “Observation gives me evidence, but not full human truth.”
7. **Tightening:** “When Grete is gone, the system I relied on no longer reaches the person who needs help.”

This serves the Lifelines pillars:

- **Empatisk nysgjerrighet:** stuck behavior becomes more understandable through evidence, not labels.
- **Tilfredsstillende vekst:** small casework discoveries unlock more appropriate actions.
- **Humoristisk kontrast:** welfare-state seriousness meets awkward concrete life: phone calls, coffee, unopened post, clippings, rent, and polite reports that do not quite cover the mess.

## Surfaces

### 1. Case Desk

The Case Desk is the default opening surface.

Initial contents:

- One document: `Bekymringsmelding`.
- One small Frank figurine standing on or near the desk.
- No broad dashboard.
- No phone-practice controls yet.
- One obvious first objective: `Etabler kontakt med Grete`.

Interaction:

- Player clicks Frank.
- Frank action menu opens.
- At first, only one action is available: `Ring Grete`.
- The action is free or nearly free: `0 terninger`, `0 tid`, or otherwise clearly tutorial-light.

Desk should feel like a place where documents create goals. It should not become a quest log. It should eventually support Roottrees-like evidence play, but the first iteration can be hardcoded and linear as long as the seams are correct.

### 2. Frank action scenes

Frank is the player’s first instrument in the world.

For the Grete call:

- Show Frank on the phone, either on the desk as a small animated/posed figurine or in a focused call panel.
- Obscure the conversation. Use chat bubbles, symbols, ellipses, sound-shape marks, or selected fragments rather than full transcript.
- Preserve the load-bearing Grete idea: she is reachable, protective, and mediating.
- Do not overexplain Elling.

Possible visual language:

```text
Frank: [phone icon] [small speech bubble]
Grete: [warm bubble] [coffee cup?] [doorway?]
Elling: absent / small shadow / not in call
```

The important part is that the player sees Frank made contact, but does not receive omniscient dialogue.

### 3. Documents and reports

After the Grete call, spawn a new desk document:

- `Frankrapport · Første kontakt`
- includes cautious observations and recommendations.

Frank reports should always separate:

1. **Observed fact** — what Frank saw/heard/did.
2. **Professional interpretation** — what this might mean.
3. **Recommended next action** — what the player can do next.
4. **Uncertainty** — what remains unknown.

Bad report shape:

```text
Elling has low self-care and needs intervention.
```

Better report shape:

```text
Grete svarte raskt og førte samtalen. Hun omtaler Elling som “en smart gutt”.
Elling deltok ikke direkte. Det er uklart om han kjenner til henvendelsen.
Anbefalt: innhent økonomisk oversikt og avtal et kort hjemmebesøk.
```

Documents should be concrete casework objects:

- `Bekymringsmelding`
- `Frankrapport`
- `Kontoutskrift / økonomisk oversikt`
- `Husleie / leiekontrakt`
- `Uåpnet post`
- `Journalutdrag` if needed later
- `Telefonregning` later, when phone ability creates a new problem

### 4. Action dice and time

After the first report, introduce resources.

Resources:

- **Action dice:** capacity/attention windows for the day.
- **Time:** day budget / Frank-hours / administrative lead time.

Do not make these abstract AP too early. The UI language should be concrete:

- `Bruk formiddagen`
- `Koster 1 terning`
- `Frank er opptatt til i morgen`
- `Svar ventes neste dag`

First available actions:

- `Be om kontoutskrift`
- `Be om journalutdrag` or hold for later if too much
- `Avtal sosialt besøk`
- `Spør Frank om vurdering`
- `Avslutt dagen`

`Be om kontoutskrift` is key. It should reveal two things:

1. If Grete dies, Elling may not afford or retain the apartment.
2. Grete may be handling practical systems Elling does not understand or cannot sustain.

The financial pressure should be legible as rent, signatures, recurring bills, unopened letters, account control, or missing income — not as a generic “financial risk +20%”.

### 5. Day advance

The player can advance when:

- resources are exhausted, or
- they choose to stop and wait.

New day restores:

- action dice,
- available time/Frank capacity,
- incoming documents/responses,
- new apartment state if time passed.

Day advance should be more than a reset button. It is how bureaucracy and domestic life move without the player watching.

Important principle:

> Waiting is an action.

If the player waits, documents may arrive, mail may pile up, Grete may compensate, or pressure may quietly grow.

### 6. Apartment view: first reveal through Frank social visit

The apartment/sim view should not be available immediately. It opens when Frank performs the first social visit.

Scene:

- Frank and Grete drink coffee or sit at kitchen table.
- Conversation is polite and obscured.
- Elling is visible but not central: in his room, at a desk, working on clippings, reading, or avoiding the shared space.
- The player can click a few objects.

Clickable evidence objects:

- Stack of mail on kitchen counter.
- Requested financial documents, if the player requested them earlier.
- Coffee cups / normal hospitality.
- Elling’s clippings / room threshold.
- Phone or hallway as later connective tissue.

The apartment should not provide full truth. It should provide situated evidence. The player should learn that the desk action changed the apartment: the document request is now physically present as mail or paperwork.

### 7. Frank returns with report

After the social visit, return to the desk.

Spawn:

- `Frankrapport · Hjemmebesøk`

Possible contents:

- Grete handled the conversation.
- Elling remained in his room / joined briefly / avoided eye contact.
- Documents are present but unopened.
- Apartment appears orderly, but Grete’s labor is visible.
- Recommendation: observe routine, clarify finances, speak to Frank, prepare for change in care situation.

Again: observed fact before interpretation.

### 8. Frank chat

Clicking Frank after the first visit should open a chat/recommendation window.

This is promising as a reusable mechanic, but it must not become an oracle. Frank should be useful precisely because he is grounded and limited.

Possible question types:

- `Hva la du merke til?`
- `Hva bør vi gjøre nå?`
- `Er Elling trygg alene?`
- `Hva vet vi ikke ennå?`
- `Kan vi vente?`

Frank answer rules:

- He cites evidence.
- He admits uncertainty.
- He suggests next actions.
- He does not diagnose.
- He can be wrong or incomplete.
- His confidence should depend on what he has actually seen.

Example:

```text
Jeg vet ikke nok ennå. Grete svarer for ham, og det kan være omsorg eller skjerming. Kanskje begge deler.
Hvis vi skal gjøre én ting nå, ville jeg sett på økonomien og om posten faktisk åpnes.
```

### 9. Observe mechanic

After the first report/chat loop, introduce observation.

Observe is not Frank. It is the state gaze.

Frank visit:

- human,
- relational,
- interpreted,
- costly in Frank-hours.

Observe:

- colder,
- fragmentary,
- precise about objects/routines,
- still not omniscient.

Observation actions can spend tokens to reveal:

- unopened mail remains untouched,
- Grete cooks / handles phone / opens letters,
- Elling returns to clippings,
- Elling avoids shared space,
- fridge/food state,
- day rhythm.

Observation should produce evidence chips or documents for the desk. It should not show inner monologue as fact. If inner-life material appears, mark it as interpretation, not state truth.

### 10. Grete death / tightening event

The first part of the game ends when Grete dies or becomes permanently unavailable.

This should not add a new game. It should break the reliability of existing systems.

Before:

- `Ring Grete` works.
- Grete helps interpret Elling.
- Documents can be routed through Grete.
- Apartment is buffered.

After:

- `Ring Grete` fails / becomes unavailable.
- `Ring Elling` is difficult or not yet possible.
- Frank visits cost more and reveal more acute problems.
- Unopened mail and finance move from concern to crisis.
- Observe shows apartment changes: food, post, routines, isolation.
- The player must choose support/build-up tiltak or relocation path.

The post-death design question is not in scope to fully build now, but the pre-death loop must set it up.

## React prototype representation

The current prototype has useful seams but the player-facing structure should be rebuilt with fresh eyes.

Keep the architectural rule:

```text
content data → pure resolver → observable store → UI presentation
```

Do not let React components own the case logic.

### Suggested domain model

Add or evolve domain concepts around the intake loop:

```ts
type LabSurface = 'desk' | 'frank_scene' | 'apartment' | 'observe';
type DayPhase = 'intake' | 'pre_death' | 'rupture' | 'after_death';

type ResourcePool = {
  day: number;
  dice: DiePoolItem[];
  timeSlots: number;
  frankHours: number;
  observeTokens: number;
};

type DocumentKind =
  | 'bekymringsmelding'
  | 'frank_report'
  | 'financial_statement'
  | 'lease_or_rent'
  | 'journal_excerpt'
  | 'mail_stack'
  | 'phone_bill';

type CaseDocument = {
  id: string;
  kind: DocumentKind;
  title: string;
  dayReceived: number;
  body: string;
  evidenceIds: string[];
  actionsUnlocked?: CaseActionId[];
};

type CaseAction = {
  id: CaseActionId;
  label: string;
  cost: { dice?: number; time?: number; frankHours?: number; observeTokens?: number };
  prerequisites: EvidenceGate[];
  result: CaseActionResult;
  unavailableReason?: string; // player language, not stat thresholds
};

type EvidenceFact = {
  id: string;
  source: 'document' | 'frank_report' | 'apartment_click' | 'observe';
  label: string;
  fact: string;
  interpretation?: string;
  confidence: 'seen' | 'reported' | 'inferred';
  anchors?: Array<'mail' | 'kitchen' | 'phone' | 'bedroom' | 'clippings' | 'finance'>;
};
```

### Suggested store responsibilities

`RootStore` or smaller stores should own:

- current day and phase,
- current surface,
- resources,
- document collection,
- evidence collection,
- available actions,
- pending action results arriving next day,
- apartment clue state,
- Frank knowledge/confidence,
- Grete availability.

Avoid overbuilding multiple stores unless the component is already painful. The next slice can still be one `RootStore` if the concepts are explicit and tested.

### Suggested flow methods

```ts
startFromConcernReport()
clickFrank()
callGreteFromConcernReport()
completeFrankScene(sceneId)
receiveDocument(documentId)
requestFinancialStatement()
scheduleSocialVisit()
advanceDay()
performSocialVisit()
collectApartmentEvidence(evidenceId)
askFrank(questionId)
unlockObserve()
observeApartment(targetId)
triggerGreteDeath()
```

These names are deliberately concrete. They are better than generic `runAttempt()` for this slice.

### Content modules

Create content files for the new slice rather than burying copy in components:

```text
src/content/intakeDocuments.ts
src/content/intakeActions.ts
src/content/frankReports.ts
src/content/apartmentClues.ts
src/content/frankQuestions.ts
```

This is where the bad writing gets fixed. Components should render, not compose prose.

### Resolver model

The next iteration does not need a complex probabilistic resolver. It needs deterministic staged consequences:

- first call always produces first Frank report;
- request financial statement schedules incoming finance document / mail object;
- social visit opens apartment and creates report;
- observation reveals selected clues;
- advance day processes pending results.

Use a pure resolver for action results:

```ts
resolveCaseAction(context, actionId): CaseActionResult
advanceCaseDay(context): DayAdvanceResult
resolveApartmentClick(context, objectId): EvidenceFact[]
resolveFrankQuestion(context, questionId): FrankAnswer
```

The dice can initially be consumed by actions without elaborate probability. Bring probabilistic dice back when the player is assigning dice to concrete practice clocks, not for first-contact bureaucracy.

## Writing rules

Player-facing copy should be plain, idiomatic Norwegian first. Small social-realist texture second.

Avoid:

- system jargon: `readiness`, `support topology`, `coverage`, `state object`, `outcome class`;
- direct English translations;
- poetic overreach;
- omniscient diagnosis;
- UI labels that sound like design notes.

Prefer:

- concrete actions: `Ring Grete`, `Be om kontoutskrift`, `Avtal hjemmebesøk`;
- casework restraint: `uklart`, `bør vurderes`, `Frank observerer`, `anbefalt neste steg`;
- objects: `post`, `kjøkkenbenk`, `telefon`, `kaffekopper`, `utklipp`, `husleie`;
- contradiction in evidence.

## Requirements for next iteration

### Functional requirements

1. App starts at Case Desk with `Bekymringsmelding` and Frank figurine.
2. Clicking Frank exposes exactly one first action: `Ring Grete`.
3. Running the call shows a short obscured phone scene, not a full transcript.
4. Completing the call adds `Frankrapport · Første kontakt` to the desk.
5. The first report unlocks action dice/time and at least two casework actions:
   - `Be om kontoutskrift / økonomisk oversikt`
   - `Avtal sosialt besøk`
6. Casework actions consume visible resources.
7. Advancing day restores daily resources and resolves pending requested documents.
8. Requested financial documents become visible both as desk/document state and as apartment/mail evidence later.
9. Social visit opens the apartment view for the first time.
10. Apartment view shows Frank+Grete polite visit, obscured conversation, Elling at distance, and clickable evidence objects.
11. Returning from visit adds `Frankrapport · Hjemmebesøk`.
12. Frank chat unlocks after the visit with at least 3 grounded questions and non-oracle answers.
13. Observe mechanic unlocks after the first visit/report loop.
14. Observe spends tokens to reveal object/routine evidence and adds it to the desk.
15. Grete death/tightening event can be triggered at the end of the slice or by day threshold.
16. After Grete death, Grete-contact actions become unavailable or fail in player-readable terms.

### Design requirements

1. The player must understand that Grete is carrying practical and social load.
2. The financial statement must create concrete concern about apartment viability.
3. The apartment must answer at least one desk action physically: requested mail/documents are visible in the room.
4. Frank reports must preserve uncertainty and cite observations.
5. Observation must feel different from Frank contact.
6. The slice must end with a sharper decision space: support/build up vs relocation, not a solved diagnosis.
7. No early omniscient diagnosis of Elling.
8. No generic dashboard replacing situated documents and objects.

### Technical requirements

1. Keep logic out of React components where possible.
2. Add content modules for documents, actions, reports, apartment clues, and Frank questions.
3. Add unit tests around the staged flow:
   - starts with only bekymringsmelding + Ring Grete;
   - call Grete creates first report;
   - request financial statement creates pending result and later document/mail clue;
   - social visit unlocks apartment and report;
   - observe spends token and creates evidence;
   - Grete death disables Grete contact.
4. Build and test pass with the repo’s Node/Vite setup.
5. Maintain or improve copy regression checks for banned system-jargon/direct-translation phrases.

## Non-goals

- No Godot cinematic implementation.
- No full post-death game.
- No full Roottrees board implementation yet.
- No general content editor.
- No multi-client system.
- No production-grade simulation.
- No LLM-driven Frank chat yet. Frank answers should be authored and state-gated.
- No fully probabilistic dice resolver for bureaucracy; save dice probability for concrete clocks and activities.

## Suggested implementation slices

### Slice A — Desk start and Grete call

- Desk renders bekymringsmelding and Frank.
- Clicking Frank shows `Ring Grete`.
- Phone scene plays with obscured bubbles/symbols.
- First Frank report appears.

This proves the opening interaction.

### Slice B — Resources and financial request

- Add day resources.
- Add `Be om kontoutskrift` and `Avtal sosialt besøk`.
- Requesting finance schedules a next-day document.
- Advancing day resolves pending results.

This proves bureaucracy/time.

### Slice C — Social visit and apartment reveal

- Social visit opens apartment.
- Show Grete+Frank coffee scene, Elling distant, clickable mail/clippings.
- Requested documents appear as mail/counter object if requested.
- Return creates Frank visit report.

This proves desk ↔ apartment connection.

### Slice D — Frank chat and observe

- Frank chat unlocks with authored questions.
- Observe tokens unlock.
- Observation creates evidence chips/documents.

This proves interpretation vs state gaze.

### Slice E — Grete death tightening

- Trigger event.
- Grete actions fail/unavailable.
- Apartment state changes.
- New decision frame appears: support/build up or relocation.

This proves the pre-death loop had stakes.

## Acceptance criteria for the SDD as a whole

- A player can describe the loop as: “I read documents, send Frank, wait for responses, inspect what changed in the apartment, and use reports/evidence to decide the next casework step.”
- The prototype makes Grete’s hidden labor visible before removing it.
- At least one player-caused desk action appears later as apartment evidence.
- The writing is plain Norwegian casework/room language, not abstract systems language.
- The implementation remains a lab: hardcoded content is fine, but the seams should support future clocks, desk evidence mapping, telescope, and phone practice.
