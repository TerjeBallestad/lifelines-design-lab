# Vedtak og Tiltak

## Design question

Does evidence-backed understanding make interventions feel earned?

## Product claim

The player should not pick support from a generic upgrade menu. They should make formal municipal decisions because the case facts and open questions justify them.

Core loop for this prototype:

```text
Sakens fakta + Åpne spørsmål → case basis → Vedtak og tiltak
```

## Scope

Add the decision layer.

### Includes

- `Vedtak og tiltak` section.
- Clear distinction between formal authorization and concrete life intervention.
- Tiltak locked/unlocked or warned by fact/question state.
- Each option previews benefit, risk, affected states, and dignity/autonomy concern.
- Small first set of choices for the housing/economy slice.

## Language distinction

### Vedtak

Formal municipal authorization. This is what the system can officially decide.

Examples:

- Start økonomisk kartlegging.
- Innvilg praktisk bistand.
- Innvilg miljøarbeideroppfølging.
- Vurder verge.
- Avvent / innhent mer informasjon.
- Midlertidig oppfølging ved Gretes sykdom.

### Tiltak

Concrete thing entering Elling's life.

Examples:

- Ukentlig postgjennomgang med Frank.
- Økonomisk oversikt: husleie først.
- Kalender / husleiepåminnelse.
- La Grete fortsette inntil videre.
- Verge-vurdering.
- Praktisk avlastning.

This split matters: welfare-state language produces vedtak, but life changes through tiltak.

## Initial decision set

### Stabilizing options

- Request økonomisk oversikt.
- Contact housing office / landlord.
- Weekly mail review with Frank.
- Temporary rent deadline follow-up.

### Capacity-building options

- Teach one bounded mail routine.
- Pay one bill with support.
- Build kitchen calendar / rent reminder.

### Risky or brittle options

- Let Grete continue handling it.
- Ask Frank to handle everything informally.
- Verge assessment.
- More intrusive housing/service assessment.

None of these should be framed as pure upgrades.

## Option preview format

Each option should show:

- intended benefit
- likely risk
- required facts / unanswered questions
- affected clocks or states
- dignity/autonomy concern
- why this may be too early, too late, too weak, or too intrusive

Example:

```text
Ukentlig postgjennomgang med Frank
Benefit: lowers immediate mail/economy risk.
Risk: Elling may experience it as loss of control if Frank opens letters without asking.
Requires: evidence that mail is blocking rent/economy chain.
Concern: stabilizes, but does not by itself teach Elling the routine.
```

## Design pressure

Every useful intervention should also create a possible cost, dependency, or new problem.

Examples:

- Weekly post review reduces risk but may reduce autonomy.
- Verge assessment is bureaucratically legible but may be overkill and damage trust.
- Letting Grete continue keeps peace now but preserves the failure point.
- Teaching a routine builds capacity but can fail if attempted before trust exists.

## Out of scope

- Rich consequence simulation.
- Full service tree.
- Permanent ending states.
- Multiple cases.
- Detailed Norwegian legal fidelity beyond the playable slice.

## Success criteria

- Player sees why some tiltak are unavailable, premature, or unwise.
- Different interventions represent different philosophies:
  - replace Grete
  - train Elling
  - stabilize crisis
  - over-bureaucratize
  - wait and gather more
- There is no obvious always-best option.
- Evidence makes the decision feel earned.

## Main risk

Tiltak become upgrades with nicer social-work names.

## Mitigation

- Pair every intervention with a tradeoff.
- Tie action availability to concrete facts, not abstract points.
- Present heavy options as bureaucratically readable, not automatically better.
- Let waiting be sometimes wise and sometimes dangerous.

## Prototype verdict question

After playtesting, ask:

> Did the player choose a tiltak because they understood the case, or because the UI told them it was the next unlocked button?
