# SDD: `Hva mangler Elling?` case board

Date: 2026-06-09
Status: Draft
Repo: `lifelines-design-lab`
Surface: Case Desk / apartment intake loop

## Source prompt

Terje wants the Lifelines mystery surface to be the municipality handling Dr. Haug's bekymringsmelding about Elling before Grete dies.

Grete has a fatal illness. Her doctor knows she is Elling's primary guardian/support person and files a bekymringsmelding to the municipality. The player is the municipality/caseworker system and must handle the concern: investigate what will actually be missing for Elling when Grete can no longer carry his everyday life, then decide what to do.

Elling has autism/anxiety-shaped functional barriers, but the player-facing truth must not be diagnosis collection. He should be able to live alone if relevant skills and scaffolds are built. He has not been pushed to develop because Grete has handled almost everything for him.

## Design stance

The playable mystery is not:

> What diagnosis does Elling have?

It is:

> Which parts of adult life are only working because Grete is silently carrying them, and which supports/skills must replace that without taking Elling's agency away?

This makes `Hva mangler Elling?` both:

1. a **mystery surface**: the player investigates categories, gathers knowledge, confirms/denies hypotheses, and fills gaps; and
2. a **goal-setting tool**: confirmed gaps become candidate tiltak, practice paths, clocks, and follow-up goals.

The board should create Roottrees-style visible unknowns, but with Lifelines' empathetic curiosity: we reconstruct a care ecology, not a culprit chain.

## Provisional exploration direction — stat sheet and skill probes

Status: provisional after `grill-with-docs` discussion. Do not treat this as the final rewrite of the SDD yet; use it as the contract for the next design-lab spike, then revise the SDD from what the spike teaches.

The appointment-attendance opener below is superseded as the immediate first proof. Appointment attendance still works as a later tiltak/training routine, but it is not strong enough as the opening mystery before the player understands Grete's load-bearing role.

The next exploration should test whether `Hva mangler Elling?` works better as an RPG-like character stat sheet / skill menu:

- Click Elling to see broad skills, initially dominated by `???` because the municipality has not observed him directly.
- Click Grete to see mostly known competent skill levels from previous correspondence and case knowledge.
- Use the same broad 3-domain / 12-skill taxonomy as `lifelines-core-loop`:
  - Self-Sufficiency: Housekeeping, Cooking, Routine, Planning
  - Social: Social Courage, Conversation, Assertiveness, Emotional Regulation
  - Inner Life: Self-Expression, Structured Writing, Creativity, Reflection
- Keep the Skill / Mastery split from core-loop:
  - Skill = broad capability.
  - Mastery = activity-specific proficiency built by doing a concrete activity repeatedly.
- Frank-mediated action-dice probes should reveal Elling's current broad skill levels and the support conditions around them.
- Later tiltak/training should build activity-specific Mastery first, and only later justify raising a broad Skill level.
- A bad probe should not make Elling obey badly or simply fail. It should risk a funny defensive tirade, retreat, or trust/pressure cost while still producing useful evidence about conditions and failure shape.

Exploration question:

> Can an RPG-like character stat sheet make municipal diagnostic work funny, legible, and humane, while action-dice probes reveal broad skill levels without turning Elling into a diagnosis sheet?

Pass condition for the spike:

- Grete comparison makes hidden infrastructure obvious: the household has competence, but much of it sits in the person who is dying.
- Elling's `???` stats create curiosity rather than contempt.
- One Frank probe uses an action die to test the situation around a suspected skill, not to randomize Elling's underlying ability.
- A negative probe outcome produces a defensive tirade or retreat and still gives actionable evidence.
- One follow-up training/tiltak option becomes legible as a next step, without requiring the whole training system.

## Core experience goal

The player should gradually revise their view of Grete:

1. **Initial read:** Grete is overprotective and holding Elling back.
2. **Investigation read:** Grete is hidden infrastructure: calendar, translator, food system, financial buffer, emotional regulator, phone interface, and crisis prevention.
3. **Mature read:** both are true. Her care has protected Elling and prevented skill development. The humane task is to transfer load before her death.

The player should feel the decision pressure between:

- replacing Grete with services, which is safer but may preserve dependency; and
- helping Elling replace parts of Grete's role himself, which is slower, riskier, but creates satisfying growth.

## Case opening

### Incoming document

`Bekymringsmelding fra Dr. Haug`

Plain-language summary:

> Grete Ballestad has a fatal illness. She is the primary support for her adult son Elling. Dr. Haug is concerned that Elling may not be able to maintain necessary daily life, health follow-up, housing, and contact with services if Grete becomes unavailable.

The document should be restrained and clinical. It should not solve the case for the player.

Possible wording:

> Mor opplyser at hun bistår med praktiske gjøremål, økonomisk oversikt og kontakt med tjenester. Pasienten fremstår sårbar ved bortfall av pårørende. Anbefaler kommunal kartlegging av funksjon, hjelpebehov og overgangstiltak.

### First playable objective

`Etabler hva Grete faktisk gjør for Elling.`

Sub-objectives:

- Contact Grete.
- Send Frank on first social visit.
- Observe one concrete apartment detail.
- Ask Frank what the detail may mean.
- Add first confirmed/uncertain board entries.

## The case board

Working title: **Hva mangler Elling?**

Each row is a life-domain slot. Each slot can hold evidence, confidence, and planning state.

### Domain statuses

Use statuses like:

- `Ukjent`
- `Selvstendig`
- `Kan med påminnelse`
- `Kan med øving`
- `Grete gjør dette nå`
- `Trenger formell støtte`
- `Akutt risiko`

These are functional labels, not diagnoses.

### Suggested domains

| Domain | Player-facing question | Evidence examples | Candidate goals / tiltak |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mat | How does food actually happen? | Empty fridge, Grete-prepped containers, receipts, missed meals | Matkasse, matombringing, shopping practice, meal plan |
| Hygiene | What routines happen without prompting? | Bathroom state, laundry smell, Frank observation, Elling denial | Prompt routine, morning checklist, practical assistance |
| Vask | Who makes clean clothes happen? | Hidden laundry basket, Grete's schedule note, no detergent | Vaskepose, laundry practice, weekly routine |
| Renhold | Can the apartment stay safe? | Trash, dishes, blocked surfaces, fire risk | Light home help, room-by-room routine |
| Søvn/rytme | Does the day start without Grete? | Drawn curtains, missed morning, Grete wake-up calls | Alarm scaffold, evening plan, low-stress morning script |
| Medisin/helse | Is health follow-up self-managed? | Pill box, prescription dates, Dr. Haug note | Hjemmesykepleie, dispenser, GP follow-up |
| Post/papirer | Does bureaucracy enter his life? | Unopened NAV/municipal letters, piles by door | Paperwork session, mail-opening routine, document translation |
| Økonomi/bolig | Can he keep the apartment? | Rent shortfall, Grete pension subsidy, unpaid bills | Økonomisk oversikt, benefits review, housing support |
| Avtaler | Can he attend appointments? | Missed appointment, calendar in Grete's handwriting | Appointment practice, transport plan, reminder chain |
| Transport | Can he get there alone? | Unused bus card, route anxiety, taxi receipts | Bus route practice, transport service, travel script |
| Telefon | Can he contact services? | Script card by phone, avoided calls, Grete calling for him | Phone practice, script, Frank-supported call |
| Sosial kontakt | Is there anyone besides Grete? | Neighbour sightings, isolation, activity refusal | Støttekontakt, low-pressure activity, neighbour role |
| Krisehåndtering | What happens when something goes wrong? | No plan, panic pattern, emergency confusion | Trygghetsalarm, crisis card, trusted contact |

## Evidence model

Each evidence item should answer one of four roles:

1. **Confirms independence** — Elling can do this reliably.
2. **Confirms Grete dependency** — the function works only because Grete performs/prompts it.
3. **Confirms trainable gap** — Elling can learn if the task is scaffolded.
4. **Confirms formal support need** — the gap is not suitable for skill-building alone right now.

Evidence should remain concrete:

- object in apartment
- Frank report
- Grete quote
- Elling quote
- Dr. Haug clarification
- municipal document
- clock movement / missed event

Avoid floating abstract tags as first-class evidence. `Anxiety` is not evidence. `Elling stands by the phone for 90 seconds, reads the first line of the script, then retreats when Frank shifts closer` is evidence.

## Hypothesis loop

The board should support Blake Manor-style hypotheses, but about need and function rather than guilt.

Example hypothesis:

> Elling understands appointments, but Grete normally prepares the chain: reminder, bus check, script for what to say, and recovery afterwards. Without that chain, he freezes.

The player confirms/denies by gathering evidence across domains:

- calendar handwriting
- Grete phone call
- unused bus card
- Frank's observation
- Elling's own explanation

Correct conclusions unlock better tiltak. Wrong/partial conclusions unlock brittle tiltak.

## Tiltak classes

### 1. Immediate stabilisation

These prevent collapse but do not necessarily build capacity.

- Matkasse / matombringing
- Vaskepose
- Light home help
- Medication reminder / dispenser
- Trygghetsalarm
- Calendar/reminder system
- Economy/rent review

### 2. Skill-building scaffolds

These are the main growth moves.

- Practice taking the bus to one appointment
- Practice one grocery route
- Practice opening one letter with support
- Practice first phone sentence with script
- Build weekly laundry routine
- Build one meal routine
- Build anxiety recovery script
- Low-pressure social activity with exit option

### 3. Network/social supports

- Clarify neighbour role
- Støttekontakt
- Day activity / activity group
- Transition meeting while Grete is still able
- Pårørendestøtte for Grete

### 4. Heavy / bureaucratically readable options

These should be present, but not score as automatically better.

- Verge
- Omsorgsbolig
- More intrusive municipal service package
- Institution-like placement

The game should not reward heavier tiltak as a higher tier. The best answer is the least intrusive support that makes life safe and creates real capacity.

## Clocks

The case board should connect to clocks.

### Grete availability clock

Stages:

1. Grete handles everything.
2. Grete tires / forgets small things.
3. Grete cancels support tasks.
4. Grete is hospitalized or unavailable.
5. Grete dies.

### Domain clocks

Potential clocks:

- Food routine
- Rent/housing risk
- Appointment attendance
- Laundry/hygiene
- Mail/paperwork backlog
- Phone avoidance
- Social isolation
- Trust in municipality
- Grete burden

Player actions can stabilize one clock while worsening another. Example: Frank calls for Elling and secures the appointment, but phone skill does not grow and dependency remains.

## Superseded first slice recommendation

This section is retained as historical design context. Appointment attendance is now treated as a later tiltak/training routine, not the immediate first proof.

Prototype one routine first: **appointment attendance**.

Case question:

> Why can't Elling get to an appointment without Grete?

This routine touches many domains without requiring the whole adult-life board:

- phone
- calendar
- anxiety/transition
- transport
- Grete prompting
- Frank support
- official paperwork
- player-visible success/failure

Target loop:

1. Read Dr. Haug's bekymringsmelding.
2. Call Grete.
3. Send Frank on social visit.
4. Observe one detail: calendar, phone script, bus card, unopened appointment letter.
5. Add evidence to `Hva mangler Elling?`.
6. Form hypothesis about the broken appointment chain.
7. Choose one tiltak:
   - Frank handles the call.
   - Grete continues handling it.
   - Appointment practice with script + bus check.
   - Transport service only.
8. See effect on clocks and board confidence.

## UI / implementation implications

### Case Desk

- Add a board panel labelled `Hva mangler Elling?`.
- Show domain rows with status chips and confidence.
- Clicking a domain shows evidence and possible next questions.
- Newly discovered evidence can mark a domain `Ukjent → Grete gjør dette nå`, `Ukjent → Kan med øving`, etc.

### Apartment

- Observations should produce evidence chips tied to domains.
- Example: `Kalender med Gretes håndskrift` updates `Avtaler` and maybe `Transport`.
- Example: `Telefonmanus ved siden av apparatet` updates `Telefon` and `Avtaler`.

### Frank interaction

Frank should not be an omniscient hint system. He should help translate observed room details into functional questions.

Pattern:

> Player: Did you notice the calendar?
>
> Frank: Yes. The appointment is written clearly, but the handwriting is Grete's. That may mean the calendar works only when she keeps it alive.

### Copy guardrails

Prefer:

- `Hva gjør Grete her?`
- `Hva skjer når hun ikke kan?`
- `Kan Elling lære dette med støtte?`
- `Hva må kommunen bære midlertidig?`

Avoid:

- `Autism trait identified`
- `Dependency score optimized`
- `Push Elling outside`
- `Normalize Elling`

## Acceptance criteria

A first useful implementation proves:

- [ ] The player can see a `Hva mangler Elling?` case board with at least 5 domains.
- [ ] At least 3 concrete apartment/desk evidence items update board domains.
- [ ] At least 1 domain can be marked as `Grete gjør dette nå`.
- [ ] At least 1 domain can be marked as `Kan med øving`.
- [ ] At least 1 chosen tiltak changes both a board state and a visible/logged outcome.
- [ ] The board supports a small hypothesis about appointment attendance.
- [ ] Copy frames Elling in functional/self-care terms, not diagnostic collection.

## Open design questions

1. Should the board be visible from the first minute, or unlocked after Dr. Haug's report and Grete call?
2. Should board entries be player-placed, auto-filed, or a hybrid?
3. How wrong can the player be before the system corrects them?
4. Should domains have binary status, confidence levels, or conflicting evidence?
5. How much of Grete's illness clock should be visible versus inferred through missed calls and changed reports?
6. Does the player represent one caseworker, Frank's municipal office, or an abstract municipality desk?

## Non-goals

- Do not turn the board into a DSM checklist.
- Do not make independence mean no support.
- Do not make heavier services an automatic win condition.
- Do not simulate all adult-life domains before one routine works.
- Do not reduce Grete to an obstacle. She is competent, loving, afraid, and load-bearing.

## Design verdict

`Hva mangler Elling?` is the right next mystery surface because it turns invisible care into structured player knowledge. It gives the player something concrete to investigate, a reason to revisit the apartment, and a way to convert observations into humane tiltak.

The former smallest-slice recommendation was appointment attendance. Current provisional direction is to explore the stat-sheet / skill-probe loop first, then revise this SDD from spike evidence.
