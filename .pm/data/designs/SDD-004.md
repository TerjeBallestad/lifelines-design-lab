# Desk Evidence Loop

## Design question

Does reading case documents and collecting authored evidence feel like meaningful municipal casework?

## Product claim

The player should feel like they are noticing case-relevant details in ordinary documents, not looting glowing clues. The first prototype should prove the simplest version of the Lifelines desk loop:

```text
Pulten → document reading → authored evidence marking → Sakens fakta
```

## Scope

Build the desk as a document-reading and fact-gathering space.

### Includes

- Pult / desk view with physical case documents.
- Clickable documents that open in a reader modal or side pane.
- Authored collectable evidence phrases inside documents.
- Spyglass / marker affordance over collectable phrases.
- Collected facts appear in `Sakens fakta`.
- Basic fact metadata:
  - source document
  - fact category
  - short fact text
  - optional original quote

### Initial fact categories

- Dokument
- Observasjon
- Samtale
- Økonomi
- Rutine
- Risiko
- Ressurs

The categories are only for readability. Do not turn this into spreadsheet admin.

## First slice content

### Documents

- Dr. Haug bekymringsmelding
- Grete call note
- Frank first visit report
- Bank statement
- Rent warning

### Example collectable facts

- Grete betaler husleien.
- Elling åpner ikke post alene.
- Kalenderen er skrevet av Grete.
- Husleien er betalt sent.
- Frank observerte uåpnede brev ved døren.
- Elling kan lese avsender på konvolutter.
- Grete sier Elling er en smart gutt.

## Interaction model

Start with authored hotspots, not full arbitrary highlighting.

Recommended first implementation:

1. Player opens a document.
2. Relevant phrases have authored evidence anchors.
3. Hovering gives a subtle spyglass / marker affordance.
4. Clicking or dragging marks the phrase.
5. The phrase creates a fact chip in `Sakens fakta`.
6. Already-collected evidence is visibly marked in the document.

This should present as highlighting, but behave as authored collection. Full arbitrary Roottrees-style text selection is a later luxury.

## Out of scope

- Arbitrary freeform text highlighting.
- Natural-language parsing of highlighted text.
- Full canvas graph interactions.
- Åpne spørsmål.
- Vedtak/tiltak.
- Apartment consequence simulation.
- Clocks.

## Success criteria

- Player understands that documents contain playable evidence.
- Collecting facts feels like noticing something meaningful.
- `Sakens fakta` becomes a useful mental inventory.
- Hotspots do not feel like cheap hidden-object pixel hunting.
- Documents are short enough that the player reads them, not scans for cursor changes.

## Main risk

The evidence collection becomes mechanical: hover until the cursor changes.

## Mitigation

- Keep documents short and written with strong signal.
- Make collectable phrases semantically obvious.
- Let visual layout and wording invite attention before the cursor confirms it.
- Avoid too many collectables per document.

## Prototype verdict question

After playtesting, ask:

> Did the player feel like they discovered case facts, or like they clicked predefined loot?
