# Desk Evidence Loop

## Design question

Does reading case documents and collecting authored evidence feel like meaningful municipal casework?

## Product claim

The player should feel like they are noticing case-relevant details in ordinary documents, not looting glowing clues. The first prototype should prove the simplest version of the Lifelines desk loop:

```text
Pulten → document reading → authored evidence marking → Sakens fakta
```

## Scope

Build the desk as a document-reading and fact-gathering space.

### Includes

- Pult / desk view with physical case documents.
- Clickable documents that open in a reader modal or side pane.
- Authored collectable evidence phrases inside documents.
- Spyglass / marker affordance over collectable phrases.
- Collected facts appear in `Sakens fakta`.
- Basic fact metadata:
  - source document
  - fact category
  - short fact text
  - optional original quote

### Initial fact categories

- Dokument
- Observasjon
- Samtale
- Økonomi
- Rutine
- Risiko
- Ressurs

The categories are only for readability. Do not turn this into spreadsheet admin.

## First slice content

### Documents

First proof should use three documents, not the eventual full five-document case set:

- Dr. Haug bekymringsmelding / clinical referral frame
- Bank statement or equivalent economy record
- Rent warning / late-payment document

Use `/Users/godstemning/dev/lifelines-core-loop/wireframes/case_session_spike_v3.html` as a document-register reference, especially the `SAKSDOKUMENTER` section, while keeping this slice smaller.

### Example collectable facts

- Grete betaler husleien.
- Elling åpner ikke post alene.
- Kalenderen er skrevet av Grete.
- Husleien er betalt sent.
- Frank observerte uåpnede brev ved døren.
- Elling kan lese avsender på konvolutter.
- Grete sier Elling er en smart gutt.

## Interaction model

Start with authored hotspots, not full arbitrary highlighting.

Recommended first implementation:

1. Player opens a document.
2. Relevant phrases have authored evidence anchors.
3. Hovering an authored phrase gives a subtle spyglass / marker affordance.
4. After a short reading delay, uncollected authored phrases may receive a faint document-like underline or margin mark.
5. Clicking the phrase lifts it as evidence and marks it visually.
6. The phrase creates a fact chip in `Sakens fakta`.
7. Already-collected evidence is visibly marked in the document.

This should present as highlighting, but behave as authored collection. Full arbitrary Roottrees-style text selection is a later luxury.


## Decision: facts auto-file, player interprets

Resolved during grill session: SDD-004 should not make the player manually drag every fact into the obvious bucket. Auto-filing a collected fact to its relevant case domain/question removes boring filing work. The player's first meaningful move should be interpreting what the fact may imply and deciding whether to investigate, stabilize, or act.

Updated target loop:

```text
Pulten → document reading → authored evidence marking → Sakens fakta → auto-linked domain/question → provisional interpretation
```

Initial interpretation choices should stay tiny and concrete, for example:

- `Grete bærer økonomien.`
- `Elling trenger praktisk påminnelse.`
- `Dette er ikke nok til å vurdere bolig.`
- `Be Frank undersøke videre.`

This is not the full `Åpne spørsmål` or hypothesis system. It is the smallest step that makes evidence collection feel like municipal casework rather than hidden-object looting.


## Decision: simulation answers brittle interpretations

Resolved during grill session: the player should be in charge of provisional interpretation and later decisions, while the simulation answers whether that interpretation was humane, sufficient, brittle, or reckless. The game should avoid immediate right/wrong judgement for case reasoning. Feedback should arrive through downstream behavior, Frank reports, document changes, unhealthy routines, clock movement, and visible apartment consequences.

Wrong or partial readings are allowed, but they should usually be authored as plausible municipal overreach, underreach, or premature certainty rather than nonsense quiz answers. A deliberately reckless option can exist when it creates useful dark comedy and exposes system boundaries, but it must not become the main loop.

Feedback contract:

- Do not label an interpretation as `correct` or `wrong` at selection time.
- Let interpretations unlock or bias next actions, probes, and tiltak.
- Let brittle interpretations produce brittle consequences: dependency preserved, unhealthy behavior, avoidant routines, increased Grete load, rent/letter/appointment clocks worsening, or Frank returning with a report that the chosen explanation did not cover the observed facts.
- When the simulation pushes back, name the concrete mismatch: `Dette forklarer ikke husleierestansen`, not `Wrong answer`.

This keeps the desk loop closer to casework than puzzle grading.


## Boundary: no simulation answer in SDD-004

Resolved during grill session: SDD-004 should record the interpretation contract, but not prototype the downstream simulation answer yet. Frank follow-up notes, clocks, tiltak consequences, apartment behavior, and judgement of brittle interpretations belong in SDD-005/SDD-006 or later.

For this slice, the job is to prove that document reading, authored evidence marking, fact collection, auto-linked domains/questions, and provisional interpretation feel like the start of municipal casework. Do not sneak in a fake consequence system just to close the loop early.


## Decision: interpretations become working case notes

Resolved during grill session: because SDD-004 holds off on consequence feedback, the interpretation step must still create visible commitment. A provisional interpretation should become an `Arbeidshypotese` note in the case file, backed by the collected fact(s) that prompted it.

Example:

```text
Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien
Evidence: Grete betaler husleien; Husleien er betalt sent
Status: Foreløpig
```

This gives the slice a desk-local payoff: the player sees the case file structure forming from documents and evidence. Interpretations are not just lightweight tags, and they are not resolved judgements. They are provisional, evidence-backed working notes.

Constraint: an `Arbeidshypotese` can only be created from at least one collected fact. The player should not be able to author free-floating opinions before evidence exists.


## Decision: hypothesis voice is system-authored Case Desk

Resolved during grill session: `Arbeidshypotese` notes in SDD-004 should be written in neutral Case Desk language, not Frank's voice and not freeform player prose. The player chooses from prepared interpretations; the system turns that choice into restrained municipal case language.

Frank remains a field/report/interview voice. Future slices may let the player discuss collected facts or working hypotheses with Frank or clients, similar to Blake Manor interviews, but those conversations should be separate from the authored case-note voice.

Example case-note voice:

```text
Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien
Foreløpig vurdering: Husleie og betalinger ser ut til å fungere gjennom Gretes system. Må sjekkes mot Ellings inntekt, faste utgifter og posthåndtering.
```

This preserves the player fantasy: the player is building the municipal case file, not merely receiving Frank's conclusions.


## Decision: discussion hooks only, scoped by character

Resolved during grill session: SDD-004 should not implement chat/interview affordances, but collected facts and working hypotheses should carry future discussion hooks. `discussable` should not be a boolean; it should be a character-scoped list, because not every fact or hypothesis should be discussable with every person.

Conceptual shape:

```text
fact: grete_pays_rent
source: bank_statement
domain: Økonomi/bolig
discussable_with: [Frank, Grete]

hypothesis: grete_carries_economy
status: Foreløpig
backed_by: [grete_pays_rent, rent_paid_late]
discussable_with: [Frank, Grete]
```

For SDD-004, this is a data/content hook only. The slice should make the evidence and hypotheses ready for later Blake Manor-like interviews without adding the conversation UI yet.


## Decision: no document-level reading prompts

Resolved during grill session: SDD-004 should not attach explicit reading prompts to each document. A prompt beside every document makes the desk feel like a worksheet and weakens the fiction that the player is reading ordinary case material.

The slice can accept some cursor-sweeping risk. If a player refuses to read, the prototype should not over-explain itself into a tutorial. The better guardrail is strong document writing and a case context that makes facts feel worth noticing.

Possible future direction: facts may become liftable only when they relate to an open case question, but SDD-004 should not depend on the full `Åpne spørsmål` system. Some documents may themselves introduce questions, so hard-gating all fact collection behind pre-existing questions would be too rigid for this slice.


## Decision: delayed subtle evidence affordance

Resolved during grill session: hotspots should not be fully visible immediately, and they should not stay purely invisible until hover. The first interaction should preserve document reading: when the player opens a document, evidence anchors behave like normal text until hovered, where the spyglass/marker affordance appears and the phrase can be lifted. After a short delay, such as about 10 seconds, uncollected evidence phrases may gain a subtle document-like underline or margin mark.

This creates a compromise:

- early readers can notice and lift facts by reading and hovering naturally;
- stuck or scanning players get a gentle affordance after they have had time to read;
- the document does not open looking like highlighted homework.

Hard guardrails:

- No videogame glow.
- No category colors inside the document.
- No immediate full highlighting of all evidence anchors.
- The delayed affordance should look like paper/casework markup: pencil underline, faint margin bracket, or similar.
- Highlighter-yellow is allowed after the player lifts a fact, as the collected-state mark.


## Decision: click-to-lift first implementation

Resolved during grill session: SDD-004 should use click-to-lift as the first implementation. Drag/highlight gestures are a later luxury. The interaction should still present visually as document marking, but the mechanical input should stay simple.

Flow:

```text
hover authored phrase → spyglass/marker appears → click → phrase becomes marked → fact chip appears in Sakens fakta
```

After lifting:

- the phrase remains visibly marked as collected;
- the player gets a short notification that a fact was added;
- `Sakens fakta` receives the fact chip in a separate evidence-canvas surface;
- the linked domain/question is stored as metadata and shown in that evidence canvas;
- the interpretation step appears when authored content thresholds/clusters say the current fact set supports one.

Do not spend this slice on drag-selection edge cases before the evidence loop is proven.


## Decision: authored interpretation thresholds

Resolved during grill session: interpretation prompts should not appear after every lifted fact by default. SDD-004 should use content-authored thresholds or clusters so the desk does not become `click fact → answer modal` repetition.

Each evidence anchor may define one of three interpretation behaviors:

- no interpretation yet;
- unlock one interpretation immediately because the fact is strong enough on its own;
- contribute to an interpretation cluster that unlocks when enough related facts are collected.

Conceptual example:

```text
fact: grete_pays_rent
contributes_to: grete_carries_economy

fact: rent_paid_late
contributes_to: grete_carries_economy

cluster: grete_carries_economy
threshold: 2 required facts
unlocks: Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien
```

A strong fact can still unlock a hypothesis immediately when that creates better pacing, but the default should avoid modal spam and let the case file form from meaningful evidence clusters.


## Decision: first slice uses three documents and one hypothesis cluster

Resolved during grill session: reduce the first proof to three documents and one earned `Arbeidshypotese` cluster. Do not build five documents just because the broader case will eventually need them. The goal is to prove the full evidence loop once, cleanly.

Minimum content target:

1. `Dr. Haug bekymringsmelding` / clinical referral frame
   - establishes why the municipality is reading the case at all;
   - may include one liftable fact about Grete carrying practical/economic/contact work.
2. Economy document, such as bank statement or rent-related record
   - fact: Grete contributes to or organizes rent/payment;
   - contributes to `grete_carries_economy`.
3. Rent warning / late-payment document
   - fact: rent has been paid late or housing risk is emerging;
   - contributes to `grete_carries_economy`;
   - completing the cluster unlocks `Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien`.

Reference source: `/Users/godstemning/dev/lifelines-core-loop/wireframes/case_session_spike_v3.html`, especially the `SAKSDOKUMENTER` section and its five visually distinct document registers. Borrow its document-register discipline, visual style, restrained Norwegian case-document tone, and highlighter-marked read-file rewards, not its full five-document scope. Useful patterns include clinical-municipal reports, Q/A interview texture, archive stamps, highlighted read-file rewards, and distinct document layouts.

The sharper slice is three documents that make one working hypothesis feel earned, not five documents with many facts.


## Decision: use wireframe visual/content style and highlighter-yellow after lift

Resolved during grill session: `case_session_spike_v3.html` is not only a content/register reference; its visual style is also desirable. SDD-004 should keep the delayed subtle affordance before collection, but once a fact is lifted, the collected phrase may become highlighter-yellow like the wireframe's marked read-file rewards.

Style contract:

- before lift: normal text, hover spyglass/marker, then delayed faint underline or margin mark;
- after lift: collected evidence may use highlighter-yellow marking;
- avoid immediate yellow highlights before the player acts;
- preserve the wireframe's restrained Norwegian case-document tone, stamps, archive/clinical/interview registers, and paper UI texture.

The design risk is not yellow itself; it is yellow before the player has done the noticing.


## Decision: Sakens fakta is separate evidence canvas with notifications

Resolved during grill session: `Sakens fakta` should not be a persistent right sidebar in SDD-004. Treat it more like Blake Manor's evidence canvas: a separate window/surface the player opens when they want to review evidence and working hypotheses. During document reading, the player receives a short notification when a fact or hypothesis is added.

Notification pattern before a hypothesis threshold is met:

```text
Faktum lagt til · Økonomi/bolig
Grete betaler husleien
```

When a threshold unlocks a working hypothesis:

```text
Arbeidshypotese låst opp
Grete bærer økonomien
```

This keeps the document-reading surface clean and preserves the feeling of collecting evidence into a larger case board without forcing a split-pane layout. The notification must be legible and satisfying, but it should not become immediate correctness feedback.

Layout contract for SDD-004:

- document viewer remains the primary surface while reading;
- lifted facts trigger a notification/toast;
- notifications are clickable and open `Sakens fakta`;
- ignoring notifications keeps the player in the document flow;
- a persistent evidence-canvas button/icon shows unread/new count;
- `Sakens fakta` / `Arbeidshypoteser` live in a separate evidence-canvas window or tab;
- opening that canvas shows collected fact chips, their linked domains/questions, provisional hypotheses, backing evidence, and `discussable_with` hooks where relevant;
- the first canvas is structured/read-only even if it borrows canvas-like visual language.


## Decision: notifications reveal domain before hypothesis threshold

Resolved during grill session: fact notifications should not reveal the future hypothesis path before the authored threshold is met. Before threshold, show the collected fact and its domain/question context. Only when the cluster threshold is met should the notification name the unlocked `Arbeidshypotese`.

Before threshold:

```text
Faktum lagt til · Økonomi/bolig
Grete betaler husleien
```

On threshold:

```text
Arbeidshypotese låst opp
Grete bærer økonomien
```

This gives immediate feedback without spoiling the interpretation cluster too early.


## Decision: clickable notifications and unread evidence count

Resolved during grill session: fact/hypothesis notifications should be clickable and should open `Sakens fakta`, but the evidence canvas should not auto-open. The player must be able to continue reading uninterrupted.

UX contract:

- lifting a fact shows a notification/toast;
- clicking the notification opens `Sakens fakta`;
- ignoring the notification keeps the player in the document;
- a persistent evidence-canvas button/icon should show an unread/new count so the canvas remains discoverable after notifications fade.

This gives `Sakens fakta` presence without turning every fact lift into a forced modal interruption.


## Decision: evidence canvas is structured/read-only in first slice

Resolved during grill session: the first `Sakens fakta` evidence canvas should be canvas-like in presentation but structured and read-only in behavior. Do not implement freeform node placement, line drawing, or manual evidence-board editing in SDD-004.

First-slice canvas shape:

```text
Økonomi/bolig
  Faktum: Grete betaler husleien
  Faktum: Husleien er betalt sent

Arbeidshypotese
  Grete bærer økonomien
  Bygger på: [Grete betaler husleien] [Husleien er betalt sent]
  Status: Foreløpig
```

The surface may borrow Blake Manor-like visual language: paper cards, scraps, columns, spatial grouping. Mechanically, it is review-only for this slice. Freeform canvas arrangement is out of scope until the evidence loop itself is proven.

## Out of scope

- Arbitrary freeform text highlighting.
- Natural-language parsing of highlighted text.
- Full canvas graph interactions or freeform evidence-board editing.
- Åpne spørsmål.
- Vedtak/tiltak.
- Apartment consequence simulation.
- Clocks.

## Success criteria

- Player understands that documents contain playable evidence.
- Collecting facts feels like noticing something meaningful.
- Lifted facts produce satisfying Blake Manor-like notifications and land in a separate `Sakens fakta` evidence-canvas surface.
- Hotspots do not feel like cheap hidden-object pixel hunting.
- Documents are short enough that the player reads them, not scans for cursor changes.
- Collected facts and `Arbeidshypotese` notes can declare `discussable_with` character hooks for later interview/chat slices, without exposing chat UI in this slice.

## Main risk

The evidence collection becomes mechanical: hover until the cursor changes.

## Mitigation

- Keep documents short and written with strong signal.
- Make collectable phrases semantically obvious.
- Let visual layout and wording invite attention before the cursor confirms it.
- Avoid too many collectables per document.

## Prototype verdict question

After playtesting, ask:

> Did the player feel like they were building a municipal case file from ordinary documents?

Supporting questions:

1. Did the player read the documents, or wait for / scan for affordances?
2. Did lifting a fact feel like noticing something, not looting?
3. Did the notification make the evidence feel safely captured without interrupting reading?
4. Did `Sakens fakta` feel like a case board worth opening?
5. Did `Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien` feel earned from the rent/economy facts?
6. Did the player understand that the hypothesis was provisional, not a correct-answer stamp?
