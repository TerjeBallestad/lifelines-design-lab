# Åpne spørsmål

## Design question

Do collected facts naturally produce open case questions the player wants to answer?

## Product claim

`Sakens fakta` is not enough by itself. Facts become gameplay when they expose uncertainty. `Åpne spørsmål` should turn case facts into live municipal questions that guide what the player investigates next.

Resolved during grill session: **SDD-005 is the question-formation slice, not the first tiltak/consequence slice.** It should prove that uncertainty itself is playable before the lab asks whether a formal decision, tiltak, clock movement, or apartment consequence was humane or brittle.

Core loop for this prototype:

```text
Collected facts → open questions → possible hypotheses → justified next case action
```

The payoff is not `tiltak unlocked`. The payoff is: “I know enough to ask a sharper next question, request one missing document, or send Frank to check one concrete thing.”

## Scope

Add the inquiry layer between facts and decisions.

### Includes

- `Åpne spørsmål` pane, table, or canvas.
- Questions that appear or update from collected facts.
- Questions with visible supporting and missing facts.
- Question state/progress.
- Competing possible hypotheses before a question is settled.
- Player selection of a provisional hypothesis from authored options.
- A justified next case action such as requesting a document, asking Frank to check something, or preparing one sharper conversation hook.

### Relationship to `Arbeidshypotese`

Resolved during grill session: `Åpne spørsmål` should be the container. `Arbeidshypotese` is a provisional answer inside a question, not a separate object unlocked directly by raw fact count.

Preferred flow:

```text
Collected fact
→ Åpent spørsmål appears/changes
→ player chooses a provisional hypothesis inside that question
→ Arbeidshypotese is created with supporting facts
→ the hypothesis justifies one sharper next investigation
```

This can resemble the SDD-003 case board: a table of case questions where the player fills in a hypothesis from authored options. It can also grow toward a Blake Manor-like canvas where evidence, questions, and hypotheses are linked as nodes. The first implementation should keep the interaction small enough to test whether the question/hypothesis relationship is interesting before building full graph manipulation.

Example:

```text
Question: Hvem holder økonomien i gang?

Supporting facts:
- Grete betaler husleien.
- Husleien er betalt sent.

Possible hypotheses:
- Grete bærer betalingskjeden.
- Elling forstår økonomien, men unngår post.
- Inntekten dekker kanskje ikke bolig uten Gretes bidrag.

Chosen hypothesis:
Arbeidshypotese: Grete bærer økonomien.
```

### Question states

- Åpent
- Delvis belyst
- Foreløpig arbeidssvar
- Gir grunnlag for neste undersøkelse

These states should not imply objective truth. They mean the municipality has enough case basis to investigate more sharply. `Avklart nok for tiltak` belongs in a later slice where the player can actually make or test a formal action.

## Example questions

Initial questions for the housing/economy slice:

1. **Hvem holder økonomien i gang?**
2. **Hva skjer med posten når Grete ikke åpner den?**
3. **Hva kollapser først hvis Grete blir innlagt?**

Later questions can branch from consequences, but SDD-005 should only prepare the inquiry language:

- Kan Elling håndtere post med støtte?
- Er problemet penger, forståelse, rutine, angst eller tillit?
- Hvilke deler kan trenes?
- Hvilke deler må kommunen overta?

## Example flow

Collected facts:

- Grete betaler husleien.
- Husleien er betalt sent to ganger.
- Elling åpner ikke post alene.

Updates question:

> Hva skjer med husleien hvis Grete blir innlagt?

Possible interpretations:

- Elling does not understand the bills.
- Elling understands but avoids official mail.
- Grete's pension/subsidy is part of the apartment economy.
- The paperwork chain depends on Grete's calendar and phone work.

A good provisional answer may be layered:

> Husleien ryker kanskje ikke fordi Elling ikke forstår penger. Den kan ryke fordi betalings- og postkjeden er privat organisert rundt Grete.

That should justify one next investigation, not resolve the case:

> Be Frank se hvordan post faktisk håndteres i leiligheten.

## Question data model

Each question should support:

- title
- short description
- relevant facts
- missing facts
- possible hypotheses
- selected `Arbeidshypotese`, if any
- current inquiry state
- justified next case actions

Avoid complex graph logic in the first version. Hard-coded authored relationships are enough.

## Out of scope

- Making formal decisions.
- Applying tiltak.
- Judging whether a tiltak is humane, brittle, reckless, or sufficient.
- Apartment consequences.
- Clock movement as consequence feedback.
- Complex node-graph editing.
- Many life domains beyond the first housing/economy slice.

## Success criteria

- Player can explain what they know and what they still do not know.
- Questions feel like case uncertainty, not quest objectives.
- Facts support questions visibly.
- The player wants one more document, report, or observation to answer the question.
- Competing explanations make the player pause rather than click the only obvious route.
- Choosing a hypothesis feels like a provisional casework commitment, not answering a quiz.
- The next available action feels earned by the question, not unlocked by a checklist.

## Main risk

The questions become a checklist: collect three facts, fill bar, unlock button.

## Mitigation

- Include competing interpretations.
- Allow partially supported provisional answers.
- Keep wording grounded in casework uncertainty.
- Avoid `0/3 clues found` presentation unless it is heavily disguised by case language.
- Replace completion language with investigation language: “Hva mangler vi?” beats “Ferdig utforsket.”

## Prototype verdict question

After playtesting, ask:

> Did `Åpne spørsmål` make the player want to investigate, or did it feel like a quest log with municipal nouns?
