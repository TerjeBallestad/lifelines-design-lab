## Motivation

The emerging design law is: help is not a buff; help changes the ecology.

A good tiltak can widen life, stabilize life in a narrower form, or create a new dependency/temptation/bill. The lab needs to show this as a core rule, not as one-off flavor text.

## Scope

Add a reusable model for paired progress and complication clocks:

- progress/capacity clock: the support makes something newly possible;
- pressure/complication clock: the new affordance can become a substitute, dependency, cost, or risk;
- case evidence explains both.

Use phone as first example: phone contact progresses, while phone bill / sex-line / unbounded private calling advances as complication.

## Acceptance

- At least one clock pair renders progress and complication side by side.
- A positive/partial success can advance both clocks.
- Frank/case-file output frames the result as “new capacity + new problem,” not success/failure.
- The next decision is sharpened by the complication clock.
- The three Lifelines pillars are used as evaluator checks: empatisk nysgjerrighet, tilfredsstillende vekst, humoristisk kontrast.

## Anti-goals

- No pure buff/upgrades.
- No complication as random punishment unrelated to the new capacity.
