## Motivation

The phone slice should stop being a generic “phone mastery” meter and become a Citizen Sleeper-style practice clock rooted in the Elling/Kjell-Bjarne film beat:

Frank cannot reach them by phone, visits, says “you guys have to answer when I call,” then dummy-practices by standing next to Elling with his mobile and saying “ring ring.” Elling is indignant, eventually picks up, blurts something, hangs up, and Frank treats that ugly attempt as progress. Later Frank calls for real from another room.

The clock should make a tiny competence gain feel satisfying, while preserving the Lifelines rule: a new ability can create a new problem.

## Scope

Replace/reshape the current phone clocks into a staged `Telefontrening` practice clock:

1. dummy “ring ring” practice;
2. pickup / blurt / hang up;
3. real call while Frank is present;
4. Frank calls from another room;
5. independent phone contact.

Add a paired risk/complication clock for what the new ability opens:

- curiosity / private calling;
- sex-line temptation;
- phone bill arrives;
- contact ability now needs boundaries.

Dice bias the attempt and clock advance; they do not select deterministic authored branches.

## Acceptance

- The UI shows `Telefontrening` as a staged practice clock, not a generic skill bar.
- At least one attempt advances the practice clock through an ugly-but-real micro-success.
- At least one successful/partial-success path also advances a risk/complication clock.
- The player can read the next decision: continue practice, pause/AVVENT, add a phone rule, handle bill risk, or seek another social outlet.
- Visible copy avoids abstract “support/proximity/readiness” language and uses the actual scene logic: Frank calls, dummy practice, pickup, hang-up, bill.

## Anti-goals

- No menu where the player directly assigns Frank’s standing position as a modifier.
- No “phone fear reduced” dashboard phrasing.
- No deterministic die-face-to-scene table.
