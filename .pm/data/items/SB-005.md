## Motivation

Citizen Sleeper works because dice are placed into concrete actions with visible clocks and visible risks. For Lifelines, the die should be assigned to a specific practice step or casework move, not to a vague “run phone attempt.”

The player’s agency should be: which clock do we push, how hard, and what complication are we willing to risk?

## Scope

Turn dice slots into clock commitments:

- choose one visible clock/step, e.g. `Dummy ring-ring`, `Real call while Frank present`, `Open phone bill`, `Set phone boundary`;
- show whether the slot is safe, risky, or locked;
- show what is missing when locked as casework evidence, not system jargon;
- resolve against current practice progress, trust, prior evidence, and active complication clocks.

The resolver should receive a selected clock step / slot intent rather than only `dieFace`.

## Acceptance

- Player chooses a specific clock step before assigning a die.
- Safe/risky/locked states are visible in player language.
- Risky slots can advance both progress and complication.
- Locked slots explain the missing evidence or prerequisite: e.g. “Frank has not yet seen them answer a dummy call,” not “requires phone_mastery >= 2.”
- Tests cover at least one locked slot and one risky progress+complication outcome.

## Anti-goals

- No generic “safe/risky/locked phone activity commitments” divorced from authored life-practice steps.
- No RPG unlock phrasing.
