## Motivation

The telescope is not a therapeutic stargazing reward. In the Elling source shape, he buys it for himself after Grete’s death and uses it to watch neighbors, inventing personalities and stories for them.

That is game-rich: the telescope satisfies social need safely, which can make him stay inside. It is both coping tool and stuckness engine.

## Scope

Prototype a telescope activity clock and substitution loop:

- telescope / neighbor-watching as a visible apartment object/activity;
- progress: Elling observes neighbors, invents stories, gains safe social material;
- complication: social pressure is satisfied indoors, reducing urgency for real contact;
- case desk evidence: names/patterns he invents, curtains closing, irritation when view is blocked;
- next tiltak options: turn one observation into a harmless greeting, set a time boundary, or protect it as recovery.

## Acceptance

- Telescope activity satisfies some social pressure while also advancing an indoors/substitution risk.
- The UI makes the contradiction legible: the object helps and traps.
- Evidence is concrete: neighbors, windows, routines, invented personalities, blocked view.
- At least one next tiltak tries to convert fantasy-contact into real contact without destroying the coping tool.

## Anti-goals

- No telescope-as-stars generic wonder beat.
- No simple “social +1” reward.
